What do I do in ekoloko?
You can do tons of stuff in ekoloko. For example: You can complete quests by clicking on ekoloko residents with a question mark over their heads. Every quest you successfully complete will earn you Activity Points and Kokos (the ekolokian currency). This will allow you to move up the level ladder and improve your Eko (Your character in ekoloko). You can also meet new friends, chat with them, play single or 1 on 1 games, and buy items and clothing that will make your Eko unique. In order to understand all this to the fullest, we recommend that you click on the big question mark on the top right hand side of the screen, and go through the tutorial.

What is HC’s orientation server?
It is highly recommended for new Ekos to select server 12 (HC’s Orientation Server). This server is designed to support you during your first steps in ekoloko: You’ll get important tips, go through special fun activities with the ekoloko moderators, and meet many Eko Rangers (*), who have been around for a long time and are there to help you

How do I advance in ekoloko?
The best way to advance in ekoloko is to complete quests and play games. The quests are initiated by the Resident characters of the world. Any character in the world that has a question mark icon over its head has a new quest for you. Click on the characters, get the quests, complete them and come back for the rewards. If you have completed all the quests available to you and still havent moved up a level, check your personal menu and see how many Activity Points you still need, and earn them by playing the different games in the world. Every game you play will award you Activity Points.

I have no quests available, what should I do?
First check your quest button. The quests that can be made available for you are in the left tab. Click each one of them to see which character can assign them to you. In the middle folder you can find quests that you have not yet completed. What should you do if both folders are empty? Play games and earn enough Activity Points to enable you to advance to the next level! Once you have advanced, many new quests will be made available to you.

Where can I find games?
Games in ekoloko can be divided into three groups: 1 on 1 games, street games that can be played on the world screens (koko hoops, basketball, etc.), and personal games that are found with the ekoloko residents and can be reached through completing quests. Some games are open to you as early as level 1 (with Heathcliff and captain Dolphinfin for example) and some will be made available for you as you advance in levels. Any game that youve already earned the right to play will remain available for you to use at any time by finding the ekoloko resident through whom you reached the game.
Back To Top.



What are 1 on 1 games?
1 on 1 games are games you can play against your friends. 1 on 1 games can be earned through completing quests, and the first game, Bongo, will be earned on level 4. If you earn a 1 on 1 game, click a friend you want to play with and then click the games icon. An invitation to play will appear in the left hand corner of the screen. If you still havent earned a game, youll have to wait for an invite.

What is the meaning of all the different icons in my personal menu ?
All your personal stats are displayed in your personal menu: Your level , your ekoloko Generation , your leadership points and your Pio Points .

Level : indicates your current level. If you want to advance and move up the levels you’ll need to complete quests and accumulate activity point. The higher level you reach, the more activities, items and accessories you will have available for you.

Leadership Points : Indicates the number of leadership points you’ve accumulated. Leadership Points can be awarded to you by your fellow Ekos for actions of friendship and leadership.

ekoloko Generation : Indicates how senior you are in ekoloko. Every day you log into ekoloko you get 1 more seniority point. The higher you generation, the more fun stuff are available for you in ekoloko.

Pio Points :Ekos who join the Pioneers receive Pio Points that allows them to do certain things in ekoloko. Pio Points accumulate automatically as long as your Pioneer membership is active. When your membership expires, you will have a few grace days to renew it before your Pio Points will disappear.

What are Leadership Points and how do I get them?
Leadership Points are points that are awarded to you in ekoloko by your friends. Every Eko over level 2 has the ability to award and receive Leadership Points. Ekos may award or subtract one Leadership Point only every 48 hours. Pioneers are able to do so once every 24 hours. Ekos with lots of Leadership Points can use cool clothes and objects and will later have more possibilities available to them, such as becoming team leaders.

What’s the meaning of the transparent Pioneer Badge icon in my personal menu?
The transparent Pioneer Badge in your personal menu indicates the number of Pio Points (Pioneer Points) you have accumulated so far. More Pio Points allows you to use more items and wear more clothes. Pio Points are being accumulated automatically as long as your membership is active. When your membership expires, you will have a few days grace before your Pio Points disappear.. Don’t worry. We will be sure to remind you the time has come to renew the membership.

What is a Community Event, and how can I participate?
A community event is a quest in which the entire ekolokian community has a common goal. To complete this quest it is essential that every Eko in the community helps out. Community events often take place in ekoloko, and last about two weeks. Unique prizes are presented to the Ekos who have participated at the end of each successful community event. When a community event is in progress, a button with a diamond icon will appear on the top right hand side of the screen. Click it and you will immediately be able to take place in the event. Community events are open to all Ekos, at every level, Pioneers and Non Pioneers.

How do I buy things in stores?
You can find lots of stores throughout ekoloko: In the Jungle, In the Eko Eko Desert, at Ekoluna and even in the Lokozaurus Valley. In order to buy and sell items in stores, you first need to earn Kokos (ekoloko money) by completing quests and games, find a store and click the glowing item on the screen. (A pile of clothes, a hairdresser's chair, a counter, a dinosaur, or even a street sign). Once you've clicked the glowing store item, an easy to use Buy/Sell menu will open.

I've noticed kids giving presents on ekoloko. How do I do that?
To give gifts on ekoloko you need to open the personal items menu, pick an item of any kind (clothing, accessory, or recycling item) and click on it while holding the CTRL key down on your keyboard. This will open an easy-to-use gift menu for you. Select the items you want to gift wrap, confirm and your present will be dropped into the world.

What is the trading zone?
Generosity and cooperation are very important values at ekoloko. The best way to give or trade an item with a friend is through the trade zone. You may invite a friend to the trade zone by clicking their character, and then clicking the button. If your friend is interested in trading, he or she will accept the invitation and you will both enter the trading zone. Here you can click on the items you are willing to give and check out what your friend is willing to give in exchange. Remember! Approve the trade only if you are happy with what you are getting in exchange. The trade will be performed only if both parties click the approve button . Once you've clicked this button you will not be able to add or take items out of the trading zone. After the first approval you will be asked to confirm the deal. Take a good look at the trading area. If you are happy with the trade, click the approve button again. If you are not happy, cancel the deal and find another Eko to trade with.

What is the meaning of the little clock stamp on some of the items in ekoloko?
Some items in ekoloko have a Time Stamp on them. This means you only get to keep them for a limited amount of time. The clock on the Time Stamp count back, indicating how much time is left for you to use the item. When the time is up, the item will disappear from your inventory.

What is a pioneer?
Pioneers are an elite group of Ekos who have the responsibility of discovering and populating new regions and of protecting ekoloko, its surroundings and its community.

To support them in this important role, pioneers get to:

  • Discover and populate new regions
  • Teleport themselves to different screens through the map
  • Become ekoloko rangers
  • Purchase and put on clothes and accessories that are restricted to pioneers
  • Participate in activities that are open only to pioneers
  • Grant Leadership Points once every 24 hours
  • Eearn and play new 1on1 games
  • The right to enter the leaders lists
And last but not least:

Pioneers get to adopt cubs that got wounded or separated from their parents.

To become a pioneer you will need to:
  • Pay the membership fee
  • Sign the pioneer pledge
  • Be willing to discover, tour and populate new areas in ekoloko
  • Complete new exciting challenges, quests and adventures
  • Be strongly connected to ekoloko and its community
  • Complete a set of special quests designed specifically for pioneers

How do I take care of my cub?
Every cub has needs: Food, hygiene and entertainment. Your cub will let you know of its needs at every moment through icons above its head. Click the cub button on the left side of the chat bar, and see to your cub's needs.

Will I get to keep my cub forever?
No. Pioneers that have shown themselves worthy of taking care of a cub only get to take care of the cubs till they are ready to be freed back into the wild. There is some good news though: Once you've taken care of a small cub, you will be able to advance and take care of a bigger animal's cub.

My cub has disappeared. What happened?
There are 3 possible reasons for your cub to disappear:

  1. It has grown big enough to be released into the wild. If this is the case you will receive a balloon as a present from your cub.
  2. Your pioneer membership period has ended. If this is the case, your cub will wait for you elsewhere till you renew your membership.
  3. You've accidentally pressed the cub's "home" button on your cub menu. Your cub will reappear when you press the same button again.

Why should I have a strong password?
Your password is the only thing that prevents other people from using your character. If anyone guesses your password, they will be able to enter ekoloko with your Eko, take all of its items away and even get you banned.
Try to select a password that is hard to guess. Do not use your user name in your password and choose a combination of letters and numbers.

May I give my password to an ekoloko manager?
NO! You should never give your password to anyone! A genuine ekoloko manager will never ask you for this information. If someone asks you for this information he or she will probably use this information to break into your account.

How can I protect my password?
We have a few tips especially for you:

  1. Never ever reveal your password to anybody! Not to real world friends and not to ekoloko buddies.
  2. When you play on a computer that is not your own private one, make sure that the "remember password" button is not ticked.
  3. Dont write your password down. If you forget it, click the "forgot password" button and it will be sent to your email address.
  4. Make sure the email address that you have entered is valid and that no one else is using it.
  5. Change your password on a regular basis.

Looking out for cheats in ekoloko
Unfortunately, in ekoloko, like in real life, some kids enjoy causing trouble. The best way to protect yourself is to obey the rules of conduct: Never reveal personal information like your password, email address, home address or IM account. Children that do not observe the rules are severely punished. We also recommend that you trade items using the trading procedure and not by trading gifts.

Who are the ekoloko Rangers?
ekoloko Rangers are children that meet the ranger criteria and have extra responsibilities to help new kids in ekoloko and make sure that the rules of conduct are observed. You can identify them by the halo hovering over their heads. To become a ranger you must be a pioneer and have reached level 7 or higher, and you must demonstrate a willingness to help newcomers. All pioneers can apply through the scroll icon on the top right hand side of the screen. If you meet the criteria you will be invited to a Rangers training meeting.
